Top Secret

Exceptionally grave national security damage. Not permitted on cloud per NISPG.

Secret

Serious national security damage. Inter-ministerial restricted. Not permitted on cloud.

Category A — Sensitive

Disclosure could damage org security, national interests, disrupt govt operations or cause significant financial loss. Examples: Aadhaar, UPI, PAN, Passport, e-Courts, Tax, Voter ID.

Category B — Official use

Meant for official use only; not published except for official purpose. Examples: Welfare schemes, websites, public grievance, feedback, events data.

Open / Public

MeitY cloud selection guidelines

F. No. 9(3)/2025-EG-II dated 20 March 2026 — key provisions summarised.

Data classification (NISPG / MHA)

Mandatory prior to cloud selection

Top Secret & Secret: Not permitted to be hosted on any cloud platform per NISPG.
Category A: Unauthorised disclosure could damage organisation security, national interests, disrupt govt operations, or cause serious financial loss. Examples: Aadhaar, e-Courts, PAN, Passport, Railways, Tax, UPI, Voter ID (GoI); CCTNS, Jail, Land records, Revenue Courts, Treasury (State).
Category B: Official use only; not published except for official purpose. Limited operational disruption if compromised. Examples: Welfare schemes, websites, public grievance, feedback, events related data.

Procurement approaches (Section 4)

Per GFR 2017 — three approved routes

iNomination route — GFR 2017 Rule 204
Purchase orders placed on nomination basis. Engage: NIC (PPP hybrid cloud, co-location), State Data Centres, BSNL, C-DAC, ITI Limited, PGCIL, RailTel, TCIL and other PSUs/MeitY entities.
iiRate contracting — DIC / NICSI
DIC has pre-discovered rates from major hyperscalers via competitive tender. Rates available at dic.gov.in/notification/. Periodic re-evaluation ensures market alignment. MSPs/SIs can also be engaged.
iiiOpen RFP / GeM
Procure directly from MeitY-empanelled CSPs on GeM portal. CSP list (public cloud, VPC, GCC) available on ambud.meity.gov.in. MSPs and SIs need not be empanelled themselves.
This OM is advisory in nature and does not impose mandatory obligations. It is the prerogative of the end-user department to judiciously classify applications and data. Departments requiring assistance may constitute an internal committee with a MeitY representative.
