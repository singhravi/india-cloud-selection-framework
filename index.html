MeitY cloud selection guidelines

F. No. 9(3)/2025-EG-II — Office Memorandum dated 20 March 2026

Data classification (Section 2 — NISPG / MHA)

Mandatory prior to cloud selection

Top Secret & Secret: Not permitted to be hosted on any cloud platform per National Information Security Policy & Guidelines (NISPG) of MHA.
Category A: Unauthorised disclosure could damage organisation security, national interests, disrupt govt operations, or cause serious financial loss. GoI: Aadhaar, e-Courts, PAN, Passport, Railways, Tax, UPI, Voter ID. State: CCTNS, Jail, Land records, Revenue Courts, Treasury.
Category B: Official use only; not published except for official purpose. Limited operational disruption if compromised. Examples: Welfare schemes, websites, public grievance, feedback, events-related data.

Procurement approaches (Section 4 — GFR 2017)

Three approved routes for government departments

i Nomination route — GFR 2017 Rule 204

Purchase orders placed on nomination basis. Engage: NIC (PPP-based hybrid cloud, co-location), State Data Centres, PSUs / MeitY entities: BSNL, C-DAC, ITI Limited, PGCIL, RailTel, TCIL — on condition that such organisations have onboarded an empanelled CSP through a fair and transparent process (GeM or open RFP).

ii Rate contracting — DIC / NICSI

Digital India Corporation (DIC) has pre-discovered cloud rates of major hyperscalers through competitive tenders. These rates ensure transparency, standardisation and cost-efficiency. Periodic re-evaluation adjusts for market conditions. Rates available at dic.gov.in/notification/.

iii Open RFP / GeM

MeitY has empanelled cloud service offerings of CSPs (Private providers, PSUs, MSMEs) to provide cloud services to Government departments. Full CSP list — offering public cloud, VPC and GCC — is available on AMBUD portal (ambud.meity.gov.in). MSPs and SIs may also be engaged and are not required to be empanelled themselves.

This OM is advisory in nature and does not impose mandatory obligations on any Government department. It is the prerogative of the end-user department to judiciously classify their applications and data. Departments requiring assistance may constitute an internal committee with a MeitY representative to advise (Section 5).
